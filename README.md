# Marie Bruneau - Human Genetics, Genetic Counseling & Computational Health

## Profile

I am an EBMG-registered genetic counsellor with approximately seven years of clinical experience in human genetics. My background combines clinical genetics, family-history interpretation, genetic risk assessment, biomedical communication, and self-directed software development for genetics-oriented tools.

I am also a self-taught and highly motivated learner in computer science. I became interested in programming because I wanted to build practical tools for problems I encountered in genetics. I used AI-assisted coding tools as learning and prototyping support while keeping the biological reasoning, project direction, testing and domain decisions grounded in my own clinical genetics expertise.

My work sits at the intersection of:

- human genetics and genetic counseling;
- pedigree-based reasoning and inheritance interpretation;
- clinical and research-oriented biomedical workflows;
- self-directed R/Shiny application development;
- AI-assisted prototyping and debugging;
- practical tools for making complex genetic information easier to explore.

## Featured Project

### Genetic Pedigree Builder

Repository: [MKB07/pedigree-builder](https://github.com/MKB07/pedigree-builder)

Pedigree Builder is an R/Shiny application designed to construct, annotate and explore genetic pedigrees. It includes:

- interactive pedigree creation;
- phenotype and individual annotation;
- relationship exploration;
- kinship and inbreeding-related calculations;
- gene and GeneReviews resource exploration;
- export and reload workflows;
- defensive rendering logic for dense pedigrees and smaller screens.

The project reflects a practical bridge between genetics domain expertise and software implementation. It was developed through self-directed learning, iterative testing, documentation reading, and AI-assisted coding support.
