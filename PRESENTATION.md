# Professional Presentation

## One-Sentence Summary

Genetic counsellor and self-taught computational health builder with seven years of clinical human genetics experience and a strong focus on pedigree reasoning, biomedical communication and genetics-oriented software tools.

## Detailed Presentation

My professional background is in human genetics and genetic counseling. I have worked in clinical genetics for approximately seven years, supporting patients and clinical teams across adult genetics, pediatric genetics, oncogenetics, cardiogenetics, prenatal genetics and preconception counseling.

This work required both scientific and human skills: collecting family histories, building and interpreting pedigrees, explaining inheritance models, evaluating genetic risks, preparing multidisciplinary discussions, and translating complex biological information into clear guidance for patients and healthcare professionals.

In parallel, I developed programming skills independently because I repeatedly encountered practical needs that available tools did not address well. I became passionate about computer science through this process, especially when I saw how programming could help represent biological reasoning more clearly.

This led me to build a genetic pedigree application in R/Shiny, combining clinical genetics knowledge with interactive software design. I used AI-assisted coding tools to support learning, documentation reading, debugging and prototyping. The biological reasoning, feature priorities, interpretation logic and user-facing decisions were guided by my clinical genetics experience.

My current profile is therefore hybrid: I am not only interested in biological sciences as a theoretical field, but in how biological reasoning can be represented, explored and supported through better computational tools.
